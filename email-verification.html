Email Verification

Overview

In current process, we authenticate only the phone number through OTP and there is no check on email. Thus we are sending unsolicited emails to unintended email owners. This feature enables customer to verify/update email ID in app, so that we can ensure we are sending right communication to the right customers.

2017

UI/UX

Challenge

On communication front apart from transactional and marketing, emails are send to remind customer of their outstanding amount and also to cross verify any chargebacks claimed by the customers.

However, in our current process, there is no check on email identification of our customers. Fraudsters are randomly entering email addresses that they don't own which results in non-passengers getting emails from Grab. We are sending unsolicited emails to unintended email owners. Our bounce rate is currently at ~5% where the email IDs provided are not real and do not belong to any user. This also has implications in terms of reputational risk, PR and the social media teams have escalated quite a number of cases where users think they got hacked or spammed via emails that were registered to a grab account, but were not done by them.

User Journey Mapping

The project started with a design workshop in which we clarified user segments, conducted user journey mapping to identify painpoints and opportunities, and drafted end to end user flow.

Low-fidelity wireframes

We conducted design review using low-fidelity wireframes to get feedback from the working team, especially for engineers to understand the design scope and to plan their ahead.

Usability Testing

We conducted usability testing to assess the usability of the design, as well as to understand PAX safety/risky situation behavior, needs and expectations.
